Methodology:

[as] combines arts & philosophy aiming to reach out to young people in a meaningful way. Together with illustrator Celia Ivey, musician Steph Singer & community artist Constantina Georgiou, siblings will explore the influence of (super/anti)heros in their lives & create their own hero illustration.

Siblings’ heros will inspire real life wearable sculptures created by multidisciplinary artist Julia Schoina.

[as] activities are free, tailored & interactive. Siblings will engage in 4 creative labs culminating in a short animation documentary, a sound composition & a postcard series. [as] will conclude with an outdoor art celebration where siblings will gather in their wearable sculptures singing, dancing, or simply being there in the moment. By mixing music, sound and visual references of illustration and costume design we will create our own music as well as wearable sculptures driven by the participants’ imagination.

Philosophy practitioner Maria Prodromou will facilitate 6 dialogue sessions run as a Community of Inquiry creating space for non-disabled siblings to converse into matters that matter to them. We anticipate that these will explore the joys & challenges of their everyday lives, as well as their hopes. 2 separate sessions will engage parents/carers.

Everyone enters the project in different phases and together we form the creative process and spend time with the siblings in series of creative labs, private conversations and interviews which will ultimately lead to a tangible end-result, - an art box-reflecting the process, the needs of the participants and our shared creative input.

Personalised art boxes will be sent to every participant with a dual purpose: a standalone multi-sensory experience for the siblings to enjoy together, as well as a kit of creative materials to be used in a shared live jam session with costume design, movement and sound.

[as] will culminate in a short animation documentary, a limited-edition poster series and a sound composition in response to the entire process. The process will be documented in a report, gathering key information and learnings on how to best move forward. This will help us establish the framework for designing a year-long series of tailored workshops which will result in outdoor art interventions in participants’ neighbourhoods.
